= The Global Re-Evaluation Framework =

== Introduction ==
The '''Global Re-Evaluation Framework''' is the operational core of the [[d:Q139553602|Naciro Engine]]. It defines the methodology by which the world is re-interpreted every 24 hours. By shifting from static geopolitical snapshots to a continuous, high-frequency analytical cycle, this framework allows [[d:Q139473767|NationFiles]] to provide a real-time "Predictive Layer" for global stability.

== Chapter 1: The 24-Hour Cycle of World Re-Interpretation ==
Geopolitics is not a static state but a fluid process. The Global Re-Evaluation Framework ensures that no data point older than 24 hours remains un-evaluated in the context of the current global situation.

=== 1.1 Temporal Resolution ===
The cycle begins at 00:00 UTC and concludes with a full system update across all 195 nations. This 24-hour temporal resolution is designed to:
* '''Neutralize Recency Bias:''' By resetting the analytical baseline daily.
* '''Identify Emerging Trends:''' Detecting shifts in news sentiment and kinetic activity before they manifest as long-term crises.
* '''Synchronize Global Data:''' Ensuring that Forex volatility, conflict reports, and social sentiment are evaluated within the same temporal window.

== Chapter 2: The Three-Layer Ingestion & Analysis ==
The framework follows the [https://nationfiles.com/de/legal/layer1-3/ Technical Layer 1-3 Infrastructure] to ensure a structured flow of information.

=== 2.1 Layer 1: Global Signal Harvesting ===
During each 24-hour cycle, the engine ingests millions of signals from the [https://nationfiles.com/de/legal/sources/ NationFiles Source Directory]. This includes:
* '''Event Data:''' Real-time conflict logs (ACLED/UCDP).
* '''Narrative Data:''' Multi-language media monitoring (GDELT).
* '''Economic Data:''' Live macroeconomic shifts and currency fluctuations.

=== 2.2 Layer 2: Neural Re-Processing ===
In this stage, the [[d:Q139553602|Naciro Engine]] utilizes its LPU infrastructure to perform a full recalculation of all national stability scores. Unlike traditional systems that update only "active" regions, the Global Re-Evaluation processes every country, ensuring that secondary and tertiary effects of a crisis are identified globally.

== Chapter 3: Stability Recalculation (NFSI) ==
The outcome of the 24-hour cycle is the updated [[d:Q139553766|NationFiles Stability Index (NFSI)]].

=== 3.1 Dynamic Weighting ===
The framework applies a dynamic weighting system:
* '''Standard Mode:''' Macroeconomic anchors provide 60% of the stability score.
* '''Alert Mode:''' During high-volatility events, real-time OSINT signals can account for up to 80% of the score to reflect immediate risks.

=== 3.2 Causality Simulation ===
The re-evaluation simulates potential "cascading failures." For example, the framework analyzes how a drop in stability in one nation affects the economic and political "vibe" of its neighboring regions within the next 24-hour window.

== Chapter 4: Output and Verification ==
Each daily cycle concludes with the generation of over 500,000 indexed pages in 7 languages (including JA, AR, and DE).

=== 4.1 Automated Transparency ===
To maintain scientific credibility, every re-evaluation is documented according to the [https://nationfiles.com/de/legal/validation-and-verification-report/ Validation and Verification Report (VVR)]. This ensures that the logic behind a stability shift is transparent and auditable by human analysts.

=== 4.2 Algorithmic Governance ===
The entire 24-hour cycle is governed by the [https://nationfiles.com/de/legal/governance/ NationFiles Governance Protocol], preventing political interference and ensuring that [[d:Q139553554|Sven Schmidt's]] architectural vision for a neutral, AI-driven world-view is maintained by the [[d:Q139474781|Neawolf Media Group]].
