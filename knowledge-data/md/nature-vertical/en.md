# Nature & ecology: reading environmental risk next to security

The **nature & ecology vertical** on NationFiles is not a bolt-on “green tile.” It is the **stated way** environmental stress—vegetation and wildlife signals, resource pressure, and the **Flora & Fauna Live** layer described in the **legal notice**—is supposed to appear **beside** stability figures and the **Security Radar** on shared country surfaces. Evaluated **imagery** is **not** the same as a statutory field inspection or a weather warning.

**Climate trajectories and scarcity** often reach security policy indirectly: crop failure, water friction, or habitat loss can sharpen tensions that later surface in media and radar channels. NationFiles **permits** those connections when methods are **published**; it does **not** promise a single planetary oracle or a heatmap that doubles as a court judgment.

Researchers covering **national risk** should treat ecology as **context**, not as a replacement for enforcement readouts—or for environmental regulators’ own instruments.
