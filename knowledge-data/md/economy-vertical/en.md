# Economy vertical: macro in context, not a hidden oracle

NationFiles’ **economy vertical** is mainly a **framing** device: it tells readers how **PPP-based income comparators**, **governance scores**, **stability context**, and **FX cross-reads** belong on the same **country** surface. That is different from campaign copy that compresses crises into a single catchy trajectory.

**Fiscal policy** appears where public series support it—debt, tax, labour market lines shown alongside the **PPI** bundle documented in the knowledge graph. Missing values usually trace to **source coverage**, not a deliberate blackout.

**Trade stability** here means reading **external-sector** indicators without conflating **PPP ranks** with **spot FX** drama. **Naciro** supplies refreshed exchange-rate context; it does **not** substitute the PPI’s published World Bank lineage or replace **official** national-account interpretation.

Stories about post-crisis **recovery** need discipline. NationFiles ties forward-looking language to **release calendars**, **snapshot timestamps**, and the disclaimers referenced in PPI and export documentation—not to a proprietary macro path smuggled into **NFKG**.
