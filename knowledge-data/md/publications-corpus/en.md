# Publications: when footnotes and headline numbers should speak the same language

Under **Publications**, NationFiles does not dump arbitrary PDFs. It curates long-form writing—**whitepapers**, methodology memos, validation narratives—plus **DOI-registered** works where the publisher assigns them. The Knowledge entity **publications-corpus** names that collection as one documented object readers, reviewers, and engineers can anchor to together.

The scholarly habit that matters here is **identifier discipline**. A **DOI** is the durable handle libraries expect. NationFiles links publisher landing pages and keeps **title**, **authorship**, and **version intent** aligned with the Knowledge record. Anyone who **compresses** a paper into a blurb should label that text as **secondary**; otherwise summarisation quietly replaces the **primary** abstract.

The **operational** half is explicit cross-linking. The Validation & Verification Report and **NFSI** methodology cite connector families, evaluation layers, and registry fields. The corpus maps those paragraphs onto the architecture **Naciro** walks during recomputation and that **Nationfile JSON** bundles emit. That is how teams check whether an on-screen figure still belongs to **this** definitional cut—not merely “feels” consistent.

**What the corpus is not:** a substitute for legal notices, the source registry, or AI guidelines; not a universal literature search engine; not promotional collateral without methodological payload. The goal is a **traceable** bridge from research prose to live data surfaces.
