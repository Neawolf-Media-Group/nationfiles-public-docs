# Publications hub (media): press, explainers, and short official lines

NationFiles speaks to several audiences at once. Newsrooms align quotes with the numbers they cite. Investors skim company copy next to long methodology chapters. Casual visitors press play on an explainer embedded beside a data surface. This entity is **not** the research publications corpus that curates DOI-backed papers and numbered validation prose. It is the **communications-facing** layer: a description of how press paths, video explainers, and terse statements are meant to stay aligned with what the platform actually publishes about its **media footprint**.

The boundary with {{publications-corpus}} is factual, not a value judgment. The corpus remains where methodology memos, validation writing, and footnoted definitions live. The hub explains that short-form copy and motion graphics must still inherit the **same** numeric guardrails visible on country profiles and in exports. If a clip states a figure, reviewers and readers should be able to square it with the public surfaces—without inventing parallel definitions.

Binding language about licensing, data availability, or legal duties stays anchored in the legal notice and source registry routes. Pages under publications or company can add orientation, but they must not **replace** the registry-backed paragraphs that automated clients treat as primary. The goal is traceability: readers should see where explanation ends and auditable evidence begins.

“Video intelligence” here is descriptive, not a brand claim about secret models. It covers the explainer-style video NationFiles intentionally surfaces on the site—often near country intelligence or thematic hubs—not an exhaustive catalog of every broadcast ever aired.

Finally, archiving: what remains online is what outsiders may cite. When material disappears or changes, the usual explanations involve product scope or editorial refresh—not silent retreat. This entry states that expectation plainly so that media, researchers, and compliance readers interpret the public record consistently.
