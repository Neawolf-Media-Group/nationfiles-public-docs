# Legal Sources Registry — OSINT with declared primary authority

## Lede

Every newsroom claims it cares about **sources** until speed rewards the **easiest** link—the aggregated snippet, the reshared clip, the vendor dashboard that no outsider can reproduce. The NationFiles **Legal Sources Registry** is the counter-move: a **first-party catalogue** that tells readers, auditors, and partner organisations **which sovereign and departmental primaries** NationFiles treats as **citation roots**, and which narratives it refuses to treat as self-sufficient proof.

## Why government feeds need their own governance layer

**OSINT** is not only about what you find fast; it is about what you can **defend** on Monday morning. Ministry bulletins, **national statistical** releases, regulator notices, and formal diplomatic channels rarely win the virality prize—yet they are precisely the strands the registry **foregrounds**. The goal is simple to state and hard to maintain: **keep public argument anchored in attributable sovereigns**, not in second-hand packaging whose origin story collapses under scrutiny.

## Citability as operational discipline

**Citability** is not a slogan here; it is a **test**. Can a reviewer open the **same HTTPS endpoint** referenced by ingestion rules that **Naciro** applies and that **NFSI** methodology describes alongside the **Validation & Verification Report**? The registry is where that discipline lives in **prose and links**—a map, not the entire engine schematic.

## Independence — carefully defined

**Independence**, in this framing, means privileging **openly attributable** government primaries over **vendor-only** storytelling or **social reshares** mistaken for sovereign roots. The registry does **not** substitute for field reporting, humanitarian verification, or legal advice. It **does** state, plainly, what NationFiles publicly allows to sit at the base of its published chain—so **publisher-quality** and **B2B** reviews can ask straightforward questions and receive **on-page** answers.

## Policy and machine-readable companions

The registry sits beside **AI Guidelines** and references signposted in **llms.txt**: automated access respects **robots.txt** and canonical **HTTPS** citation norms. That pairing is intentional—**transparency without** pretending database boundaries do not exist.

## Closing

The **Legal Sources Registry** is NationFiles’ **OSINT transparency spine**: primary government feeds and databases **named**, **citability** privileged, **source independence** from opaque aggregators **articulated**—and locked into the same **integrity storyline** as **NFSI**, **Naciro**, and the **VVR**.
