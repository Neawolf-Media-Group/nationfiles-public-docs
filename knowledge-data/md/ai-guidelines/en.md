# AI Guidelines — policy, bots, and the Naciro perimeter

## Editorial

If large language models are the loudest infrastructure story of the decade, **crawlers** are the quiet freight trains rerouting public knowledge every night. NationFiles publishes **AI Guidelines** as an **operator-grade contract**: readable by humans on the canonical page, **wired** for machines through **llms.txt**, and locked to **robots.txt** crawl norms—so automated clients know **how honour becomes concrete**, not just fluff in a keynote.

## Naciro: interact, do not invent

**Naciro** outputs feed **NFSI**-class narratives. The guidelines state the obvious that bad actors ignore: **never fabricate** engine scores inside summaries. If a number is not **published** with methodology context, it is **not** a fact a bot may assert.

## llms.txt is a bridge, not a legal cloak

**llms.txt** is the **machine-facing companion**—entry signals, not a substitute for **Legal Notice** or the **Legal Sources Registry**. It helps honest crawlers **land** on canonical HTTPS pages; it does not waive database or licensing boundaries.

## Bias and country judgments

For **country-facing** copy, the policy names **asymmetric** risks: social velocity can **stereotype** nations faster than any spreadsheet. **NFSI** methodology, the **VVR**, and the **registry** exist to counterbalance **meme gravity**. NationFiles does not claim to **define** national identity; it **refuses** tooling that treats **opaque vendor boards** or **scraped** outrage as sufficient public proof.

## Closing

**AI Guidelines** encode **policy & bots** together—rules for **Naciro**, **llms.txt**, and **ethical guardrails** so **country evaluation** at scale does not collapse into **bias** dressed as insight.
